AHERI

...promoting and supporting networking among higher education researchers in Africa, aimed at improving higher education and to advocate for implementation of the research findings to influence decision making within the higher education sector and towards general development in African states.

Rationale

Presently the demand for higher education in Africa far outweighs the spaces available in the higher education institutions. There are ongoing efforts to address this challenge by the individual national systems and institutions. In addition to these efforts a smaller proportion of students get placements in institutions abroad. However these efforts still fall short of the demand for higher education. This initiative is motivated by the existence of similar institutes and centres within developed nations and the contributions towards development in those nations. A key element worth of note, is that all developed countries and the countries with emerging economies embraced research in higher education as a field of study and as a profession prior to their growth phases.

Geographical focus

AHERI will eventually focus on Africa as opposed to individual countries because the initiative would be more sustainable. This is due to the fact that the overhead cost of individual country or institutional investment into the initiative would be prohibitive. The countries in this region are also interconnected economically and socially hence gains made in one country can easily be lost or have reduced effect if others are not taken into effect.
