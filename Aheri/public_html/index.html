Research in higher education.

Aheri carries out relevant research aimed at improving higher education, publish and document the results and to advocate for implementation of the research findings to influence decision making within the higher education sector and towards general development in African states.

Teaching and training.

Through research Aheri works towards promoting the use of modern methods and technology in teaching and training at universities in Africa. We also are committed to developing sustainable and regular training programmes on management & research in higher education.

Orientation and placement

Develop innovative strategies and programmes to assist graduates have satisfactory transition from school to work and for students going abroad for studies to ensure they are successful.

Information management

Aheri works towards the development of comprehensive data base of the national education system to assist in making informed policy decision through of technology.

Rationale

Presently the demand for higher education in Africa far outweighs the spaces available in the higher education institutions. There are ongoing efforts to address this challenge by the individual national systems and institutions. However these efforts still fall short of the huge demand for higher education. The situation has resulted from the disproportionate increase of primary and secondary enrolment and completion rates compared to higher education development rates.

Thematic areas

AHERI's activities are categorized in the following thematic areas:

  • Governance Structures and Reform within higher education institutions
  • Quality Assurance systems and structures within higher education system
  • Accessibility to higher education by all segments of the society
  • Match and Mismatch of higher education qualification in the job market.
  • Research Funding and its effect to development of higher education and the society

Geographical focus

AHERI is based in Kenya and had its first engagement with Kenyan higher education institutions. AHERI will eventually focus on Africa regional blocks as opposed to individual countries because the initiative would be more sustainable. This is due to the fact that the countries in the region are interconnected economically and socially hence gains made in one country can easily be lost or have reduced effect if other countries are not involved.
